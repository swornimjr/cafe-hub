# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Cafe staff at two locations (Atrium & Cleanskin). Two main contexts:
- **Boss / team leader on desktop** — planning rosters for the week, approving stock requests, reviewing today's staffing at a glance. Sitting down, focused, needs density and control.
- **Staff on mobile** — quick check of their shift before or during work. Phone in hand, one thumb, needs immediate clarity with zero cognitive load.

Both contexts matter equally. The UI must be comfortable on a 390px phone and a 1280px laptop without feeling like a compromise in either direction.

## Product Purpose

Cafe Hub replaces manual roster spreadsheets, WhatsApp stock requests, and verbal handoffs with a single tool that covers the full daily operations loop: scheduling, stock ordering, menu/recipe reference, and announcements. Success looks like staff opening the app and knowing exactly what they need to know in under 10 seconds.

## Brand Personality

Clean, professional, efficient. The UI is a tool, not an experience — it gets out of the way. But "clean" here means purposeful and sharp, not cold or minimal. The café's warmth comes through in the color (forest green accent, dark espresso tones) and copy voice, not in decorative UI chrome.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS clones** (Notion, Linear, every-startup-looks-the-same): no cold gray systems, no excessive whitespace-as-design, no "designed for the design community" minimalism. This tool is used by baristas and shift workers, not PMs.
- **Dense POS / hospitality software** (Square, old Lightspeed): no button grids, no information overload, no cramped touchscreen-era layouts.
- **Corporate HR tools** (Deputy, Tanda, Humanforce): no lifeless gray enterprise aesthetic, no 8px font tables, no "compliance software" feel.

## Design Principles

1. **Immediate clarity over comprehensiveness** — every screen should answer the user's most likely question before they have to look for it. Dashboard shows today, not a summary of everything.
2. **Density earns its place** — the boss view can be denser than the staff view, but neither should feel cluttered. If content has to fight for space, restructure rather than shrink.
3. **Consistent, not uniform** — the two stores (Atrium green, Cleanskin dark slate) have distinct identities within the same design system. Lean into that contrast; don't flatten it.
4. **Mobile is first-class** — not an afterthought. Layout, touch targets, and information hierarchy are designed mobile-up.
5. **The café is the brand** — DM Sans, forest green (#16A34A), espresso dark (#1E293B). These are locked. Work with them, not around them.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline. Minimum 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large/bold text. All interactive elements have visible focus states. Touch targets minimum 44×44px. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected. No color-only state communication (always pair with icon or text label).
